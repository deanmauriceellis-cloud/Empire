Key user decisions made during design:
- Construction units DESTROYED when killed (not captured) — makes them precious
- Buildings CAPTURED when enemy takes the tile — creates high-value targets
- Captured cities REVERT to captor's tech level — no inherited bonuses
- Turn end is MANUAL (Enter key) — triggers Economy Review Screen
- Opponent economy INVISIBLE (fog of war only, debug toggle for dev)
- Auto-AI for BOTH players from turn 1 — with manual click override
- River maps should support defensive gameplay — bridges, fortifications, ranged combat

#### New Bombard Mechanic
- Units/structures with range > 1 use BOMBARD action (ranged attack, no return damage)
- Cannot capture cities (only melee armies can)
- Costs 1 move point per shot
- Structures auto-bombard during behavior phase
- Units that bombard: Artillery (2), Missile Cruiser (3), Anti-Air (2), Coastal Battery (2), Artillery Fort (3), SAM Site (3)

#### Resource System
- 3 natural resources: Ore, Oil, Textile (from map deposits via mines/wells/farms)
- 4 derived resources: Science, Health, Electronics, War Research (from city upgrades)
- Starting: 150 ore, 100 oil, 150 textile, 0 derived
- Resources consumed when production STARTS — city stalls if can't afford
- ~20 deposits per standard map (~7 each type), fair placement near both players

#### Turn Flow
1. PLANNING PHASE: Player explores map, clicks units/cities, adjusts behaviors
2. Player presses ENTER → Economy Review Screen (mandatory, tabbed: Events/Resources/Cities/Tech/Construction/Buildings)
3. Player makes strategic decisions (production, construction orders, tech review)
4. Player confirms → EXECUTION PHASE: AI moves all units (both players), combat resolves, resources tick, tech recalculates
5. Next turn

#### River Dynamics
Rivers become strategic chokepoints with defensive structures:
- Bridges allow army crossing but are destroyable
- Coastal Batteries deny naval passage (range 2 covers river width)
- Artillery Forts bombard across river (range 3)
- Minefields on banks punish amphibious landings
- Sea Mines block transport approaches
- Anti-Air denies fighter recon
- Breaking a fortified river requires combined arms: Artillery to shell defenses, Special Forces to infiltrate, AWACS for intel, Missile Cruiser for fire support, OR flank around via undefended crossing

## Important Design Decisions & Rationale

### Why 150 Starting Resources (Not 1000)
User originally proposed 1000 of each. Analysis showed 1000 ore = 200 armies funded, making economy irrelevant for 500+ turns. 150 creates pressure by turn 40-50, forcing deposit investment. Math: 150 ore / 5 per army = 30 armies from seed. With 2 cities producing armies every 2.5 turns, seed runs out around turn 75.

### Why Linear Tech Scaling (Not Exponential 1/2/4/8)
User proposed exponential growth. Analysis showed level 7 = 128 points/turn from a single building — game-breaking snowball. Linear scaling (each level costs N×10 more, gives steady +1 bonus) keeps games competitive. Max level 5 = 150 accumulated points.

### Why Buildings Attach to Cities (Not Arbitrary Tiles)
User proposed buildings anywhere. Analysis showed infinite placement options create AI nightmare. City upgrade slots (max 4) give AI clear decisions and make captured cities more valuable.

### Why Construction Units Destroyed (Not Captured)
User decision. Makes construction units "gold" — precious, hard to replace (10 turns build time, costs 10 ore + 5 textile). Creates escort gameplay (send army with your engineer).

### Why Auto-AI for Both Players
Key gameplay transformation: human becomes general, not sergeant. AI handles all unit movement via existing behavior system (Explore, Aggressive, GoTo, etc.). Human controls strategy: what to build, where to invest, which units to redirect. Click override preserved for tactical intervention.

### Why Bombard Cannot Capture Cities
Prevents artillery/missile cruiser from winning the game alone. You MUST have armies on the ground to capture cities. Ranged units soften defenses; armies take territory. Creates combined-arms gameplay.

### Why River Dynamics Matter
River War maps with defensive structures create "fortress Europe" gameplay: building a fortified river line is defensive strategy, breaking it requires combined arms and tech investment. This is the design goal — moving away from "spam armies and attack" into strategic depth.
